What We Look For

  • Originality & Contribution: Clear research question and novel insight.
  • Rigor & Reproducibility: Sound methodology, adequate experiments/theory.
  • Clarity: Well-structured writing, suitable for an interdisciplinary faculty audience.
  • Ethics: Responsible data/model use, disclosure of limitations and risks.

Article Types

  • Full Research Articles
  • Short Communications / Technical Notes
  • Survey / Tutorial Articles
  • Applied Case Studies within FACOMS focus areas

We welcome work that bridges computing and mathematical sciences and encourages replication packages, datasets, and supplementary materials where feasible.
